San Francisco's crime during
the COVID pandemic and lockdown

San Francisco has a crime registration database that is publicly accessible and is the center of this analysis. For those who are unfamiliar with this dataset, it contains millions of reported crime incidents that occurred in the city of San Francisco from 2003 to present day. It is still in use today and regularly updated. This list details the time and date of the incident, the district and exact location, category of crime, status of the case, reference number etc. Since the raw data originally has close to 100 different type of crime categories, we've simplifies the task by regrouping some and focusing on the following eight: "assault", "burglary", "weapons", "vandalism", "traffic", "fraud", "drug offenses", "sex offenses". This following analysis is based on our simplified focused crime data. Our first visual overview of the data was a multi-line time series plot showing yearly crime counts of our eight selected “crime categories” from 2003 to 2025. The year 2026 is excluded since, as of the writing of this analysis, it is still an incomplete year. This plot reveals a clear change of crime rate and pattern around the year 2020. This analysis takes a closer look at this shift in the data.

Here is a time series plot focused on the time period of interest, from mid 2019 to mid 2021. Looking at the "burglary” crime counts, the orange curve, we observe a clear rise in occurrences from March 2020 onwards. In fact, according to the National Library of Medicine (https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7808609/), the first mandatory COVID Lockdown was announced on March 16th in the San Francisco Bay Area. From there onwards people were sent to stay home leaving offices, shops, car parks, and public spaces unattended, which we suspect explains this observed raise in burglary counts.

We cross references our hypothesis across the internet and found several sources citing incidents and reports of theft.
This article from NBC Bay Area News, published early April 2020, only two weeks after the first lockdown, reports an increase in shoplifting and risky behaviors in stores. "Employees and customers at a San Francisco Walgreens are calling for additional staff, security, and protective equipment during the COVID-19 pandemic, saying shoplifting and dangerous behavior inside the store have reached new heights during the city's weeks-long stay-at-home order." https://www.nbcbayarea.com/investigations/shoplifting-risky-behavior-reach-new-heights-during-pandemic-customers-and-employees-at-sf-walgreens-say/2265713/
Ten months after the first lockdown: This article from BCS, published in January 2021, reports a raise in break ins, particularly in small local businesses under the Lockdown. "Burglaries have gone up 111% in the neighborhood during the pandemic, compared to the same period the year prior". (ref: https://www.cbsnews.com/sanfrancisco/news/pandemic-ravaged-san-francisco-small-businesses-hit-with-burglaries-and-vandalism/).
Paper published in August 2022, analyzing theft and criminal patterns in San Francisco note that commercial zones were indeed an easy target. "Several studies have found declines, in residential burglary and an increase in commercial burglary following COVID-19 lockdown orders". "Mixed land use areas may have a higher risk of commercial burglary because employees and customers were no longer present to act as capable guardians as businesses were forced to be closed during the lockdown periods". https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9387408/
