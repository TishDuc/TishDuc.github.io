Introduction For our social dada analysis assignment, my team and I have chosen to have a look at San Fransisco's crime dataset. For those who are unfamiliar with this dataset, it contains all reported crimes in San Fransisco from 2003 to today (it's still being updated and used) and lists details such as the time and date of the incident, district and exact location, category of crime, status of the case etc. We started off by plotting a very generic count plot, visually indicating the number of crime types per year, for example "assult counts for 2008", "burglary counts for 2012" etc. It quickly revealed a change of patterns around 2020. This coincides with the unique situation of the COVID pandemis and lockdown period, which is what we decided to focus on for our analysis. How does a pandemic of this size influence crime rates? What kind of changes do we see?
